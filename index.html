üìå Key Takeaways

  • IgA Nephropathy in children may rarely present with a rapidly progressive GN picture
  • Aggressive therapy is recommended for RPGN due to IgA Nephropathy
  • End-stage kidney disease may still develop despite aggressive therapy
  • Urine output may be restored even when kidney function does not significantly improve

üìö Literature Review

IgA nephropathy (IgAN) is the most common primary glomerulonephritis worldwide. While typically characterized by a slowly progressive course, approximately 5% of patients present with rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis (RPGN).

Recent studies suggest that aggressive immunosuppressive therapy combining steroids with cyclophosphamide or rituximab may improve outcomes in severe cases, particularly when initiated early.

The Oxford Classification for IgAN (MEST-C score) helps stratify risk and guide treatment decisions. This patient's score (M1 E1 S1 T2 C0) indicates mesangial hypercellularity, endocapillary hypercellularity, segmental glomerulosclerosis, and severe tubular atrophy/interstitial fibrosis, all associated with poorer prognosis.
